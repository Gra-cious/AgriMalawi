This glossary defines the standard terminology used throughout the AgriMalawi project.

---

## A

### AgriMalawi

A digital agriculture platform designed to help farmers and aspiring farmers successfully plan, budget, cultivate, and manage agricultural projects.

---

## C

### Crop

A cultivated plant grown for food, income, or other agricultural purposes.

### Crop Project

A complete step-by-step guide that enables a user to successfully cultivate a specific crop from planning through harvest and post-harvest handling.

A Crop Project includes:

- Introduction
- Land requirements
- Soil requirements
- Seed selection
- Land preparation
- Planting
- Fertilizer programme
- Irrigation schedule
- Weed management
- Pest management
- Disease management
- Labour requirements
- Equipment
- Budget
- Expected yield
- Harvest
- Post-harvest handling

---

## F

### Farmer

Any person using AgriMalawi to plan, learn, or manage agricultural activities.

### Fertilizer

A substance applied to crops to supply nutrients required for healthy growth.

---

## H

### Herbicide

A chemical used to control or eliminate weeds.

---

## I

### Input

Any resource required during crop production.

Examples include:

- Seeds
- Fertilizer
- Herbicides
- Pesticides
- Water
- Labour
- Equipment

### Irrigation

The artificial application of water to crops.

---

## P

### Pest

An organism that damages crops and reduces productivity.

### Pesticide

A chemical or biological product used to control crop pests.

### Project Budget

The estimated total cost required to complete a Crop Project.

---

## S

### Soil Type

The physical classification of soil suitable for cultivating a particular crop.

---

## U

### User

Any individual who accesses AgriMalawi, whether registered or not.

---

## V

### Variety

A specific cultivated type of a crop with distinct characteristics.

Example:

- Tomato variety
- Maize variety
- Groundnut variety

---

## W

### Weed

An unwanted plant that competes with crops for nutrients, water, sunlight, and space.

---

## Y

### Yield

The expected quantity of produce harvested from a specified area, usually measured per hectare or acre.
